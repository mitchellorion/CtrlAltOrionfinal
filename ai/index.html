Our Answer

Describe Your
Policy. We
Handle the
Rest.

A general manager describes their house tip-out policy in plain English. Our LLM configuration layer translates it into a structured, auditable rules engine. Every distribution is calculated the same way, every night, with a full explanation attached.

Example Input

"Servers tip out 3% of food sales to bussers and 1.5% to food runners. Bar keeps its own tips. Managers are excluded from pools."

That sentence becomes a reproducible rule set. Every edge case — split shifts, server-bartender combos, void comps — is handled deterministically and logged.

A Note from the Founder
"I built this because I needed it every
Saturday night at close."

I have spent years working front-of-house and back-of-house in restaurants and bars in Portland. I have done the tip-out math on my phone at 1am. I have watched arguments start because the server in section three got a different number than they expected and nobody could explain why.

The software that exists today handles the easy cases — standard pools, flat percentages, single-concept operations. The moment a venue has a non-standard policy, a split-shift edge case, or a multi-role night, you are back on the calculator. That is most venues.

The wedge is tip-out because it is the most painful, most universal, and most underserved workflow in the industry. But the real opportunity is the data layer it creates. A venue that runs tip-out through our system for six months has a distribution dataset that no forecasting product can replicate. That is the moat.

We are building this in Portland, with direct input from operators and staff. If you run a venue and want early access — or want to tell me everything that is wrong with the industry — reach out below.
